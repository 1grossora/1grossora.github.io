Some Science Stuff

My Thesis was done with the MicroBooNE detector at Fermi National Accelerator Lab (FNAL) in Batavia, Il. The detector is large Liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber (LArTPC) which is to study the properties of neutrinos, a fundamental particle of nature. I joined the collaboration at the beginning of the construction phase and was lucky to play an integral role in assembling the primary TPC system of the detector.

Being able to contribute to both construction, commissioning, and data analysis is something most graduate student unfortunately do not get to do anymore. Below shows a video of cosmic ray particles that are reconstructed with our detector.
